Pursuits · Off the clock

Five things I take
unreasonably seriously.

These are the hobbies I've been at long enough to have opinions about. They shape how I think, how I work, and — honestly — how I show up in a professional setting. Character compounds in the places you're not being graded.

01 · Bodybuilding

Reps are the
original compound
interest.

The split

Current block

Five days a week — push / pull / legs / upper / lower. Currently on a lean bulk, three years of consistent training. The BYU RB is home base; Gold's on 1400 N handles overflow. I don't miss Mondays.

The goal

This cycle

Slow, honest hypertrophy. Add 8–10 pounds this year without losing sight of form. Bench, squat, and deadlift are tracked weekly in a notes file; if the numbers aren't moving I'm not guessing why.

What it's taught me

Transferable lessons

Patience, consistency, the difference between effort and intensity, how to show up on bad days. The gym is where I learned most of what I bring into rooms that don't have barbells in them.

02 · Menswear

Clothes
are the
language.

Growing up in Allen I didn't think about it. Moving to Provo and buying my own clothes made me start paying attention.

The lane

Aesthetic

Workwear, heritage, and a little darkness. Heavy denim, leather jackets, broken-in boots, one weird thing. Not loud — built to repeat, built to last. A small rotation beats a full closet.

The rotation

What actually gets worn

A couple pairs of raw denim, a leather jacket, a workwear chore coat, knit beanies, a pair of red wing boots that are finally broken in. The point is the repetition — wearing something enough that it becomes yours.

Why it matters

Transferable lessons

Dressing well is a cheap way to signal that you care about details. In business school, in interviews, in every room where attention is a currency, presentation is a free edge you can choose to take.

03 · Skateboarding

The only sport
that teaches
you to fall.

Where I skate

Setup

Street and park. Started around the spots in Allen and Plano, picked it back up in Provo after the mission. Current setup: 8.25″ Baker deck, Independent 149 trucks, Spitfire Formula Four 99a wheels. Simple, reliable, fast.

What I'm working on

Current tricks

A clean tre flip down three stairs. Nollie heelflips. Smoother 50-50 transitions on longer ledges. I'd rather land one trick clean than roll away from five sloppy ones.

Why it matters

Transferable lessons

Skating is public failure on repeat. Thirty bails before the one good try, then the camera cuts. It trains you to stop being precious about looking bad — which is the only way to actually get good at anything.

04 · Snowboarding

Winter is the
only season
that counts.

Brighton Resort, Big Cottonwood Canyon. Hour up from Provo when the canyon's being kind.

The mountain

Home base

Brighton, ~25 days a season. Powder on weekdays if class allows, park laps on weekends. Great terrain for a snowboarder — open trees, a legit park, and no lift lines compared to Little Cottonwood.

The setup

Board / bindings / boots

Burton Custom 156, Union Strata bindings, Burton Photon BOA boots. Camber profile because I'd rather hold an edge than flail through slush. Nothing flashy — built for full runs, full mountain.

The goal

This season

Clean backside 360 off the medium kicker at Brighton's park, switch landing. And finally getting comfortable reading powder lines in the trees rather than sticking to the groomed run to the side of them.

05 · 2hollis

The loudest
thing in my
rotation.

Who it is

Hollis Frazier-Herndon

LA-based producer/rapper/singer, 21, on Interscope. Self-produces, self-directs. His catalog so far: White Tiger (2022), 2 (2023), boy (2024), star (2025). Genre-bending isn't a marketing line — it's the whole thing.

Why it matters

The hook

Every album sounds like a different artist decided to wear the same name. Rap, electronic, hyperpop, straight-ahead pop — he refuses to stay in one lane, and that's the lane. The gym playlist, the drive to Brighton, the last set — all him.

Go-to tracks

Starting points

“tell me,” “destroy me,” “flash” off star · “jeans,” “FORFEIT,” “poster boy” off 2 · and “safety” if you want the older sound. Find one and the rest of the catalog opens up.
