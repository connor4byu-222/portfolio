01 · Iron / Bodybuilding

Reps are the
cheapest therapy
I've ever paid for.

I've been lifting seriously for about three years — started right after my mission and haven't taken more than a week off since. Nothing competitive, just consistent. The gym is where I figured out what showing up looks like, and the rest of my life borrowed the habit.

Current focus: lean bulk. Five-day split, moderate volume, honest form. The BYU RB is home base when class is in session, Gold's on 1400 N when it isn't. If I'm lucky I'll be in this game for sixty more years; no reason to rush.

The current split

  1. Push — Chest / Shoulders / Triceps
    • Incline dumbbell press — 4×8
    • Flat barbell bench — 3×6
    • Seated overhead press — 3×10
    • Cable lateral raise — 4×12
    • Rope pushdown — 3×12
  2. Pull — Back / Biceps
    • Weighted pull-ups — 4×6
    • Barbell row — 4×8
    • Lat pulldown — 3×10
    • Face pull — 3×15
    • Dumbbell curl — 3×10
  3. Legs — Quads / Hams / Glutes
    • Back squat — 4×6
    • Romanian deadlift — 3×8
    • Walking lunges — 3×10 each
    • Leg curl — 3×12
    • Calf raise — 4×15
  4. Upper — volume day
    • Dumbbell bench — 4×10
    • Chest-supported row — 4×10
    • Arnold press — 3×12
    • Preacher curl + tri extension — 3×12 superset
  5. Lower — posterior focus
    • Conventional deadlift — 3×5
    • Hip thrust — 3×10
    • Front squat — 3×8
    • Leg extension — 3×15

Progress is the only metric I care about. If the bar is heavier than it was last month, I'm winning.

Loaded bar, chalk, and patience — the only gear that matters.

02 · Cloth / Menswear

Clothes are
a second language.
Worth learning to speak.

Current rotation — workwear meets leather, kept quiet.

Growing up in Texas I wore whatever was clean. Moving to Provo and actually paying for my own clothes changed that quick. Fit, fabric, and proportion were saying something — and presentation is a skill that compounds just like any other.

I'm not loud about it. My rotation's small, repeats often, and leans workwear + heritage with one thing too weird for the outfit. That last item is the point. Heavy leather, washed denim, beat-up boots. Dark, restrained, built to last.

My rules, in order

  1. Fit beats label. Every time.
  2. Shoes first, tee last. If the shoes are right, the rest has somewhere to go.
  3. Own three great pairs of jeans. Not ten okay ones.
  4. Tailors are cheaper than new clothes. Use one.
  5. One weird thing per outfit. Never two.
  6. If it needs a caption, it's doing too much.
  7. Buy less, care more. The wardrobe you keep wearing is the one that was worth buying.
03 · Street / Skateboarding

The only sport that
teaches you to
fall on camera.

Skating's the sport where thirty bails a day is a normal Tuesday. It teaches you to fail in public, get up fast, and film the one good try. That skill transfers everywhere. Business school, cold calls, bad first dates — same energy.

I grew up skating the parks around Allen and Plano and picked it back up when I landed in Provo. Mostly street and park. Current setup: an 8.25″ Baker deck on Independent 149s with Spitfire Formula Four 99a — not because I'm picky, because I've broken enough stuff to know what works.

Tricks I've got clean

Ollie, kickflip, heelflip, pop shove-it, varial flip, front 180, back 180, half-cab flip, 50-50s on ledges. Still working on: a clean tre flip down more than two stairs. It's been humbling me for six months.

Thrasher · Ishod Wair, Skater of the Year — the standard.

04 · Snow / Snowboarding

Winter is the
only season
that counts.

Hang time, helmet off, full commit — that's the whole sport.

Home mountain: Brighton Resort. Hour up from Provo if the canyon's behaving. I ride ~25 days a season, chasing powder on weekdays and running park laps on weekends when Little Cottonwood turns into a parking lot.

Setup: Burton Custom 156 with Union Strata bindings and Burton Photon BOA boots. Camber profile because I'd rather hold an edge than flail through slush. Texas kid learning to stop carving like a skier was the whole first season — second year the tool clicked.

This season's goal: a clean backside 360 off the medium kicker at Brighton's park, switch landing. Last season's lesson: keep your weight forward or the mountain will teach you the hard way.
